Introduction:

WordNet is a large, open-source, lexical database of English. Nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs are grouped into sets of cognitive synonyms (synsets), each expressing a distinct concept. Synsets are interlinked utilizing conceptual-semantic and lexical relations.

Concepts are abstract ideas or general notions in our minds, we represent them as nodes in the OMW graph which are linked to synsets in individual wordnets.

Basic Search Function:


 

The Basic Search function is used to search lemmas, which is present on the head of the concept page. A lemma is a word you find in the dictionary or wordnet. In the search function, there are two language select options. You can select the languages in which you want to search the word in the search bar. For example, if you choose English & French, and then if you search for a word, it will search that word in both languages.

Glob Patterns:

Glob patterns are used for file path expansion with the help of wildcard characters. See the OMW documentation on Glob.

Hints (tooltips):

Many elements have short explanations that will be shown if you hover over them. If you click on them, they will lead you to more detailed information. For example, if you want to know what hypernym means, you can hover your pointer over hypernym.

For obtaining more information, you may click on hypernym.

Elements of a Concept:

Elements of a concept include head, senses, definitions, examples, synset-relations, and synset-sources.

Head:

The head section contains lemma, part of speech, core synset symbol (may or may not be present), link to ili (ili Id) and definition of the lemma.


 
Part of Speech:

It implies whether the searched lemma is a noun, adjective, etc.'n' in the image above implies that the lemma is a noun.

  • Parts of Speech: Information for the parts of speech used by OMW
Core synset symbol ✪ :

You may or may not see a star inscribed in a circle next to the part of speech. Core concepts are those in the the semi-automatically compiled list of 4,960 core word senses in the Princeton WordNet (approximately the 5,000 most frequently used word senses in the British National Corpus).

  • Core synset ✪
Link to ili:

There is a unique ili ID for each word, you may hover it or may click it to get more information.

Definitions of the lemma:

Senses:

The sense is relation to other expressions in the language system. It is one of the meanings of a lemma. Senses mean all the possible meanings of a lemma. In this Senses section you can look at all the possible meanings of a lemma. Each sense of a word is in a different synset.

Definitions:

In the definitions section, you can see a statement of the exact meaning of a word.

Examples:

An example of the entered lemma will be listed here. It may or may not be available.

Synset Relations:

Here you can look up for Part Holonym, Hypernym, Hyponym, Member Holonym, Domain topic, Instance Hyponym, and other synset relations.

Restful Interface

  • Search for an ili_id with this to get the ili record (as html)
    omw/ili/concepts/ili_id
    e.g. {{ ili_link(46593, 'omw/ili/concepts/46593') }}
  • Search for an ili_id with this to get the concept (as html)
    omw/concepts/ili/ili_id
    e.g. {{ ili_ss_link(46593, 'omw/concepts/46593') }}
  • Search within a wordnet using it's project, version and synset identifier:
    omw/src/prj/ver/sysnet_id
